Personas & Storyboarding

Overview

Through this project, I attempt to step into the user's shoes and develop an in-depth understanding of their experiences using the thermostat in my home. In this process, I observe and interview three thermostat users to define personas and create a storyboard illustrating the user's journey using the product.

The Thermostat in Question

This digital thermostat gives residents and visitors of the house the ability to view and adjust the temperature. Its digital screen indicates the current temperature and the desired. It also indicates if the fan is on at any given time. Below the screen, the interface features the option to toggle between the home's heating and cooling systems as well as the option to turn both systems off. An additional toggle allows the user to select if the fan should be on or automatic. Finally, the increment and decrement buttons to the right of the screen allow the user to increase or decrease the temperature at which the thermostat is set.

General Observations

To better understand how users would interact with this interface, I observed three of my roommates use the thermostat's interface. A summary of my general observations can be found below:
  • Two users immediately used '+' and '-' buttons and completely avoided changing the system setting
  • None of the users adjusted the fan toggle. Some questioned its purpose and how it fit into the entire system
  • All three users waited until they could hear the system audibly to stop adjusting the interface
  • One user looked at interface closely, adjusted system setting, then adjusted temperature using '+' and '-' buttons

Interview Questions

Following each observation, I conducted an interview with the user to gain insight on their motives, expectations, and general experiences using the thermostat. Below you will find an overview of the interviews with three users
  1. When and why do you use this interface?
    • User 1: I don't user it a lot. I only ever use it if I'm feeling so uncomfortably hot or cold that I like wake up or something. This is typically in the middle of the night or right when I wake up in the morning.
    • User 2: I like to bundle up at night, so I turn it the system off at night— unless it’s hot out of course. Other than that, I glance at it pretty much every time I walk by it just out of curiosity, and if the temperature is uncomfortable in my room, I usually get up to change the temp.
    • User 3: I use it when I'm feeling really cold in the mornings.
  2. How was your experience using this interface?
    • User 1: I usually don't pay attention when I use it. I press the up or down buttons until I hear it turn on. Simple. The +/- buttons are intuitive and the screen is lit up, so there's no confusion about the temperature.
    • User 2: Standard. I always get confused by temperatures. I don't know if I should turn on the heat or A/C, but the temperature screen helps help.
    • User 3: It went well. I did what I needed it to and ignored the rest.
  3. What challenges did you encounter if any?
    • User 1: The "FAN" section threw me off. I don't really know what the fan's purpose is in the whole eco-system. There must be a reason why you can't completely turn it off.
    • User 2: I had to make sure it was on the right system because sometime’s I’ll put the heating system on and then lower the temperature instead of turning on the A/C then lowering the temp. I had to make sure I didn't do that. Then, I didn’t hear the air turn on, so I had lowered the temp some more to help trigger it.
    • User 3: The only thing I could see as a challenge was that I didn't understand the purpose of the "fan" portion of the thermostat.

Persona 1: Confused Carla

Confused Carla is a college student who enjoys sleeping in a cold room but finds the entire cooling and heating system confusing.

Who does Carla represnt?
Carla is representative of the users who are confused by the interface. She is confused by the difference between the heating and cooling system because the interface allows her to lower and raise the temperature on both system settings. She has trouble recognizing whether or not the system has started heating or cooling by looking at the system, so she has to listen for the sound of the system kicking on instead. She also has no idea what the fan setting is referring to.

What interface problems does Carla face?

  • Lack of indication of system starting to heat or cool
  • Ability to raise the temperature on the cooling system and lower it on the heating system.
  • Seemingly arbitrary fan option

Persona 2: Sleepy Steve

Sleepy Steve is a tired college student in a rush to get back to bed after his frigid room has woken him up.

Who does Steve represnt?
Steve is representative of users who do not pay much attention when they use the thermostat's interface. He is too tired to care because he just wants to get back to bed. All he does is push buttons until he hears the system working.

What interface problems does Steve face?

  • Steve is tired and cannot easily identify if the system is starting to heat or cool by looking at the interface, so he relies on the sound of the system working.
